Our Story

The Heart Behind Washington's Wharf

A warm, lively British fish & chips restaurant in the heart of Cumming, Georgia — where great food and good company are always on the menu.

Who We Are

Bringing Britain to Georgia

Washington's Wharf is an authentic British fish and chips restaurant nestled in Cumming City Center, opened by Albert and Amanda Reeves — a Gainesville couple with a passion for good food and community. The Reeves previously owned The Monkey Barrel restaurant on Gainesville Square and Washington Street Deli, so they know a thing or two about creating a great dining experience.

When the space formerly occupied by the beloved Wright's Fish & Chips became available, the Reeves jumped at the chance to continue the tradition the community loved — while adding their own touch: a full pub-style bar, a warm welcoming atmosphere, and a commitment to making every plate the best it can be.

"British Fish and Chips isn't just on our menu — it is our menu."

Every piece of fish is hand-battered fresh to order. Every batch of chips is cooked properly — with your choice of American style (crispy, seasoned shoestrings) or British style (thick-cut, chunky, pub-perfect). And the house-made tartar sauce? Guests drive across town just for that.

Step through the door and you'll find a space that's warm, casual, and lively — 80s hits on the speakers, a cosy bar area, friendly staff, and tables full of happy locals. It's a neighbourhood gem, and Cumming has well and truly claimed it as its own.
